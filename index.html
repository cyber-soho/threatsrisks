Course Description. This introductory course equips students to identify and represent information assets, assess internal and external threats, evaluate risk factors, and propose effective mitigation strategies. By the end of the course, students develop risk mitigation strategies based on assessed severity, likelihood, and impact on information assets.

Achievement Context

  • Entire network comprising various platforms; continuous across cybersecurity activities.
  • With/without supervision; in collaboration with analysts, network admins, and business managers.
  • Public sources: cyber‚Äëthreat newsletters; risk analysis frameworks.
  • Private sources: information asset inventory; penetration test, compliance, incident & monitoring reports; policies & standards.

Performance Criteria

  • Ethical behaviour; effective priority management; effective stakeholder communication.
  • Consider/synthesize diverse sources; accurate interpretation of available data; confidentiality of results.

Elements & Indicators

  1. Identify information assets.
    • Manage hardware/software inventory and permissions.
    • Identify critical data/services; represent physical & logical infrastructure.
  2. Identify threats.
    • Identify external actors & motivations; internal threats; analyze public sources.
  3. Identify risk factors.
    • Architecture vulnerabilities; obsolete apps/services; risk factors: material/software/organizational/human.
    • Analyze internal sources of pertinent information.
  4. Assess probability & impact.
    • Prioritize threats; assess propagation, CIA impacts, complexity, and response capability.
  5. Propose risk mitigation measures.
    • Determine appropriate measures and applicability by risk level; ensure continuity of activities.
  6. Propose eradication measures.
    • Determine preventative measures to required security/resilience; assess risks associated with preventative measures.
