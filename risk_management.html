I. Risk Management Frameworks (NIST, ISO 31000, FAIR)

A. Introduction to Risk Management Frameworks

Definition: A risk management framework (RMF) is a structured set of guidelines, processes, and standards to identify, assess, manage, and monitor risks.

Analogy: Secure your house: identify threats, estimate likelihood/impact, apply appropriate controls.

B. NIST RMF

NIST RMF: Categorize → Select → Implement → Assess → Authorize → Monitor

Useful link: NIST Risk Management (CSRC)

C. ISO 31000

Principles + Framework + Process with continual improvement.

Useful link: ISO 31000 Overview

D. FAIR

FAIR: ALE = LEF × PLM — communicate risk in monetary terms.

Useful link: FAIR Institute

II. Applying Risk Management Frameworks

A. Risk Identification

  • Workshops & Brainstorming
  • NIST-based checklists
  • Threat intel & historical incidents
  • Vulnerability scans & pen tests

B. Risk Analysis

Qualitative heatmap for triage; consider FAIR for quantification.

C. Risk Evaluation

Compare analysis to acceptance criteria; decide to accept, mitigate, avoid, or transfer.

D. Risk Treatment

  • Mitigate: Technical (MFA, EDR), administrative (policies, training), physical (locks, guards).
  • Transfer: Insurance, outsourcing.
  • Avoid: Stop risky activity.
  • Accept: If cost > benefit or risk is low.
III. Developing Risk Mitigation Plans

A. Prioritization

  • Risk level (likelihood × impact)
  • Cost/effort
  • Regulations
  • Business criticality

B. Designing Controls

Iterate with PDCA for continual improvement.

C. Monitoring & Review

  • SIEM & alerts
  • Audits & assessments
  • Vuln management
  • Threat tracking
  • KPIs: incidents, MTTD, MTTR

D. Reporting & Communication

Tailor to stakeholders; emphasize risk posture and business impact.
